Bio

I’m passionate about teaching, technology, and data science. I received my PhD in mathematics in 2020 at UIUC working in differential geometry and dynamical systems under the direction of Eugene Lerman.

I have also developed and facilitated workshops on many topics including scientific computation, data analysis, and applied linear algebra.

In the recent past, I worked at NetMath where I taught and helped develop a wide variety of online courses including Calculus I-III, applied linear algebra, differential equations, and probability. Currently, I have been working as an academic advisor in the UIUC Department of Mathematics.

Here you can find my CV, read about my teaching philosophy, and learn more about the workshops I’ve created or helped facilitate.
